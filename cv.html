ACADEMIC RESEARCH

  • BIFURCATIONS IN THE KURAMOTO MODEL WITH INERTIA
    • Advisor: Matthew S. Mizuhara, PhD
    • JAN 2020 - MAY 2021
    • Independent Study
    • The Kuramoto Model is a well-known dynamical system which has been shown to exhibit the same dynamics as any system of coupled oscillators; i.e., congregations of fireflies, systems of pacemaker cells in the heart, networks of neurons in the brain, and Josephson Junction arrays. The behavior of the original system is known, but the original analysis makes assumptions such as "all-to-all" coupling, and the internal frequencies are distributed according to a Gaussian distribution, and excludes a term for inertia. We analyzed the effect of small inertia and various network topologies on the multistability of incoherent/coherent solutions.
    • Used Matlab to run simulations of our model which can be found on GitHub.
  • FRACTAL GEOMETRY AND COMPLEX DIMENSIONS
    • Advisor: Edward K. Voskanian, PhD
    • JAN 2020 - MAY 2021
    • Guided Study
    • The Theory of Complex Dimensions offers a newly proposed definition of fractality in terms of the existence of nonreal complex dimensions, which includes the Cantor Function as opposed to the traditional definition. The Theory also exposes a surprising connection between the geometry of objects known as "Fractal Drums" and the classical Reimann Zeta Function, using carefully defined zeta functions and volume formulas. In this work we proved a formula to compute the inner-tubular volume of any finite product of fractal strings which will be used by the primary author of the theory in his own work.
    • Implemented an algorithm in python that can create images of any Self-Similar Fractal String in which the user inputs as the form of a list of scaling factors. The source code can be found on GitHub.
  • SIMULTANEOUS DIOPHANTINE APPROXIMATION VIA THE ILLL ALGORITHM
    • Advisor: Edward K. Voskanian, PhD
    • MUSE: Mentored Undergraduate Summer Experience - Summer 2020
    • Independent Study - Spring 2021
    • Implemented a library containing all necessary algorithms hosted on GitHub.
    • The continued fraction algorithm is known to give good rational approximations of any real number. Dirichlet's Approximation Theorem proves that a solution for the more general case exists. The well-known lattice basis reduction algorithm, LLL, was shown to be applicable to such case; however, LLL is not a perfect analogue of the continued fraction algorithm. A newly proposed algorithm called ILLL builds on LLL and outputs a sequence of increasingly accurate approximations with prescribed accuracy. We investigate the performance of the dyadic implementation as well as propose modifications.
